# 18. AMT And Cryptographic Extension Boundary

AMT defines address reference and safe resolution. Cryptographic systems can
prove predicates about AMT envelopes, but they do not by themselves solve
candidate completeness, source truth, or real-world deliverability.

This chapter marks the boundary between AMT, commitments, nullifiers, ZK-ready
predicates, and future audited circuits.
