# 3. Registrable Entities

A registrable entity is any entity that can safely receive a persistent address
identifier under a declared context and evidence policy.

Examples include buildings, units, entrances, lockers, ports, temporary
shelters, public institutions, delivery handoff points, islands, marine areas,
and natural or cultural places.

AMT separates entity type from expression type. A street label, QR alias, hotel
room handoff, sea name, or informal settlement name may all point to different
kinds of registrable entities.

Model hook: `formal/definitions.ts`.
