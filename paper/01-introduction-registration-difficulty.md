# 1. Introduction: Is Registration Difficult?

Registration is difficult because an address is not merely a string to be
stored. It is a reference to an entity under a context, a purpose, an evidence
policy, and a risk boundary.

AMT starts from a conservative principle: if a resolver cannot identify the
referent responsibly, it must preserve uncertainty instead of emitting a
precise identifier.

This chapter introduces the central problem: how to transform surface address
expressions into safe, evidence-bound identifiers while keeping ambiguity,
history, privacy, and social continuity visible.

Model hook: `formal/definitions.ts`, `formal/unresolvability.ts`.
