# Predicate Taxonomy

The first useful predicate families are:

1. Region membership
   - prove that an address graph node is within an allowed region.
2. Quality threshold
   - prove that evidence quality is above a verifier policy threshold.
3. Freshness and revocation
   - prove that a credential is recent and not revoked.
4. Purpose and consent scope
   - prove that a requested use is within holder consent.
5. Duplicate prevention
   - prove one-time or bounded-use without revealing the address.
6. Delivery reachability
   - prove a handoff zone or stop candidate is acceptable.
7. Postal equivalence
   - prove membership in a postal or postal-equivalent zone.

Each predicate needs a public-signal policy and a failure state.
